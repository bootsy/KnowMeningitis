Meningitis can turn your world upside down. It can strike quickly and leave people with life-changing disabilities. With this disease, even a matter of minutes can make a difference. Scroll down to understand how you can Tackle Meningitis and take steps to protect your family.

What is Meningoccal Disease?

Meningitis is an inflammation of the protective membranes lining the brain and spinal cord, and is caused by the entry of bacteria or viruses into the body. Meningitis can strike suddenly - sometimes without warning. It progresses quickly and requires immediate treatment.

Meningitis is caused by the entry of bacteria or viruses into the body. 1 in 10 people carry these bacteria and viruses in their nose or throat.

Infection can be spread by:

Coughing

Sneezing

Kissing

Sharing Utensils

Meningitis can also cause severe blood poisoning called meningococcal septicaemia and result in lifelong damage. 1 in 3 bacterial meningitis survivors may be left with long-term physical and cognitive disabilities like limb amputations and blindness

Strains of Meningitis

Meningococcal bacteria can cause meningitis, septicaemia or both3, and while this bacterial meningitis is rare it can be very serious if not treated quickly4. There are several types, or strains, of meningococcal bacteria.

There are vaccines available to protect against some strains but there is no single vaccine to protect against all

Meningitis in Ireland

In Ireland, meningococcal Group B and C cause the majority of disease5, but all of the meningococcal bacteria strains can be fatal

Who is at Risk

Babies & Toddlers

While the meningococcal bacteria can infect anyone â€“ babies and children under five are the most at risk. During the early years of life, children have an undeveloped immune system, meaning they cannot fight infection easily.

Teenagers & young people

Teenagers and young people are the second most at risk ages for contracting meningitis8. 1 in 4 15-19 year-olds carry the meningitis-causing bacteria9 in their throats which, combined with increased social interaction, means the bacteria can be passed on more easily.

Can you identify the Early Symptoms?

Symptoms of meningococcal disease can be difficult to recognise, and can easily be mistaken for a common cold or virus. Can you identify the early symptoms? Select all that apply from the list of meningitis symptoms.

  • Fever

  • Dislike of bright lights

  • Vomiting

  • Seizures

  • Stiff neck

  • Headache

  • Rapid breathing

  • Confused

  • Cold hands and feet

  • Small red blotchy rash

  • Muscle pain

In the early stages, it can be very difficult to tell the signs of meningitis apart from milder diseases and conditions. The symptoms above can appear in any order and some may not appear at all.
